## The Importance of Material Selection

A CNC machine is only as good as the material it cuts. In CNC wood carving, selecting the wrong species of wood can lead to chipping, splintering, rapid tool wear, or warping after the project is completed.

In this guide, we break down the characteristics of popular hardwoods, softwoods, and engineered woods to help you choose the perfect substrate for your next CNC carving project.

---

### Hardwoods vs. Softwoods

#### 1. Hardwoods (The Premium Choice)
Hardwoods come from broad-leaved trees and have a dense, tight cell structure. This density allows CNC routers to cut exceptionally clean details without tear-out.

* **Teak Wood**: The king of hardwoods. Known for its rich golden color, beautiful grain, and natural oils that resist moisture, decay, and termites. Ideal for luxury entrance doors and premium artifacts.
* **Oak Wood (Red & White)**: Extremely strong and heavy with a prominent grain pattern. Carves beautifully and takes stains exceptionally well. Excellent for furniture panels.
* **Maple Wood**: Very hard with a fine, uniform texture. Yields highly detailed cuts and is excellent for painted finishes or clear-coated objects.

#### 2. Softwoods (Easy to Cut, but Fragile)
Softwoods come from coniferous trees. While they are softer and faster to machine, they are prone to splintering along the grain and may require extensive hand-sanding.

* **Pine Wood**: Inexpensive and easy to machine, but highly resinous. Can gum up router bits. Best suited for simple shapes, prototyping, or rustic projects.

---

### Engineered Woods (Outstanding for Panels)

For large interior panels, wave boards, and painted furniture, engineered woods offer superior stability and cost savings over solid wood:

* **MDF (Medium-Density Fibreboard)**: The absolute best engineered wood for CNC routing. It has no grain, meaning it cuts smoothly in any direction without splintering. Once sanded and primed, it yields a flawless, ultra-smooth painted surface.
* **WPC (Wood Plastic Composite)**: A blend of wood fibres and thermoplastics. Fully waterproof, making it the premier material for bathroom screens, wet areas, and exterior ceilings.
