## What are Wave Boards?

Wave boards are textured MDF, WPC, or solid wood sheets carved with continuous, flowing wave-like patterns. When installed side-by-side, these panels align seamlessly, creating the illusion of a single, massive, undulating surface.

As interior design trends shift towards organic shapes, fluid movements, and tactile textures, wave boards have emerged as one of the most popular cladding materials for 2024.

---

### Key Applications in Contemporary Spaces

#### 1. Feature Walls in Reception Areas
First impressions matter. In corporate offices and luxury hotels, a large wave-board feature wall behind the reception desk—especially in a metallic or high-gloss Duco finish—instantly communicates modern innovation and premium quality.

#### 2. Bedroom Bed-Heads
Cladding the wall behind the bed with wave boards adds a soft, flowing texture that enhances the room's comfort and acoustic dampening, while replacing the need for traditional upholstered bed-heads.

#### 3. Ceiling Accents
Using textured wave boards on ceilings, particularly in corridors or dining areas, draws the eye upward and hides structural joints, creating a highly sophisticated, unified ceiling plane.

---

### Choosing the Right Material Substrate

* **MDF (Medium-Density Fibreboard)**: Perfect for dry interiors. Carves beautifully and yields an exceptionally smooth painted surface.
* **WPC (Wood Plastic Composite)**: The ideal choice for high-humidity areas like bathrooms, kitchens, and semi-outdoor ceilings. Fully water-resistant and termite-proof.
* **Solid Wood**: The ultimate luxury. Provides the natural beauty, depth, and unique grain patterns of teak or oak.
